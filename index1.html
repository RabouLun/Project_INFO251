About Siem Reap


The province of Siem Reap is located in northwest Cambodia. It is the most important tourist destination in Cambodia since it is the nearest city to the world-famous Angkor temples (the Angkor temple complex is north of the city). The provincial capital, also known as Siem Reap, is located in the province's south on the banks of Tonle Sap Lake, Southeast Asia's largest sweet water reservoir. The city's name literally means "Siamese defeated," referring to the Khmer Empire's 17th-century triumph against the Thai kingdom's troops.
