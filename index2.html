About Phnom Penh


Phnom Penh is Cambodia's dynamic and busy capital. At the junction of three rivers, the huge Mekong, the Bassac, and the vast Tonle Sap, lies what was formerly known as Indochina's "Gem." The capital city retains a lot of its beauty and has a lot to offer. With French colonial palaces and tree-lined boulevards amidst gigantic Angkorian buildings, it emanates a sort of provincial beauty and peacefulness. In comparison to the modernism of other Asian capitals, Phnom Penh is a real sanctuary. Visitors visiting the Kingdom of Cambodia's capital will find a fusion of Asian exotica and the legendary Cambodian warmth.

Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum

The Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum is the memorial site of the Khmer Rouge regime's S-21 interrogation and incarceration camp. It is located in the middle of Phnom Penh and commemorates a terrible moment in history in order to urge tourists to be peace messengers.

National Museum

The National Museum of Cambodia, in Phnom Penh, houses one of the world's most extensive collections of Khmer cultural items. Its encyclopedic collection spans prehistory through the post-Angkorian period and includes sculpture, pottery, and anthropological material.
